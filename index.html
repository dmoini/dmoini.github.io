About Me

Hello and welcome to my portfolio website! My name is Donovan and I am currently a software development engineer at Amazon, specifically working on the Alexa LifeCycle team which deals with the discovery and deletion (aka lifecycle) of all Alexa connected devices.

To give you a little insight about myself, I graduated from Loyola Marymount University with a Bachelor of Science in computer science and a minor in philosophy. Some of my involvements during college include the following: resident advisor, computer science teaching assistant, MAGIS Service Organization, Alpha Sigma Nu, and orientation leader.

Some facts about me include I was born and raised in the wonderful city of Los Angeles, I am a soccer fan and support Chelsea FC, I love watching movies and television shows, especially Brooklyn Nine-Nine, Rick and Morty, Bojack Horseman, and Black Mirror, and I love spending time with my friends and meeting new people.

I have enjoyed computer science and programming so much since I first started. Funny enough, it was a last minute change before I came to college to become a computer science major. Luckily enough, I have been able to pursue a field that both intrigues and challenges me on a daily basis, and I am excited to see how much more I will learn within this field.

Additionally, I am passionate about service and social justice. I have been doing service and discussing social justice topics since I began college. I hope that with these passions and my ever growing knowledge and skills in computer science, I can make a positive impact around me.

CampusGANDR

CampusGANDR embeds Personalized Normative Feedback (PNF) in a gamified mobile application to reduce defensiveness and reactance. These results will be presented in a game-like fashion so users can learn along with fellow classmates, discover more aspects of campus life, interact with their peers in a friendly environment, and size up the competition to become the top GANDRer. Casper Programming Language

Collaborated with four other LMU students to create Casper: The Friendly Programming Language. Casper is reminiscent of Python with nuanced features and syntax designed to make its meaning as transparent as possible and make programming an entirely pleasant affair.
